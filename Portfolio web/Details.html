Introduction

Nininja is a fast-paced 2D action platformer built in Unity, centered around responsive combat and fluid movement. Players take control of a nimble ninja navigating dynamic environments, engaging in close-quarters battles against intelligent enemies. The game focuses on tight controls, impactful attacks, and iterative combat design to deliver a polished and satisfying gameplay experience.

Concept

Nininja is a 2D ninja-themed game set in an obstacle-filled village where players must fight enemies, solve simple tasks, and uncover hidden secrets. The core concept focuses on fast-paced combat using shurikens and movement tactics, combined with AI opponents that chase and retreat, creating a dynamic and strategic gameplay experience.

Prototyping

Prototyping Nininja involves quickly building basic gameplay loops like player movement, combat mechanics (throwing shurikens, dodging), and simple enemy AI behaviors (chasing, attacking, retreating). Focus on testing core interactions and level flow early to refine controls, pacing, and challenge before adding polish or complex features. Keep iterations fast and feedback-driven.

Crafting Game Experiences

Working on Nininja provided me with extensive experience in multiple aspects of game development. I focused on designing and implementing responsive player controls, ensuring that movement, dodging, and combat mechanics felt intuitive and satisfying. Creating fluid combat required careful attention to timing, animation transitions, and interaction between player attack and enemy behaviors.

I also developed enemy AI systems, giving opponents simple yet effective behaviors such as chasing the player within a radius, retreating to heal, and patrolling designated areas. Balancing these mechanics taught me how AI design directly impacts gameplay pacing and player experience.
